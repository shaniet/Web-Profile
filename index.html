About Me

        Throughout my early academic career at UCLA I studied economics and never really touched on anything code related. I first discovered my passion for programming during my second year of college in my first ever data science class. As I was learning how to analyze econometric modes in R I immediately realized the impact I could make with programming. Right away I learned python and web development on my own and changed my minor to data science engineering. When I took my first “official” computer science class, I truly couldn’t believe the power I had now been given. I realized all the cool things I now had the ability to do and the resources available to learn anything I wanted. After my experiences interning as a software engineer I knew that this was it for me. My passion for software engineering is to work with extremely talented, hard working people and learn from them to be able to contribute back in any way. I think my educational background brings a unique perspective to the table and would love any opportunity to grow in this field.
